# ⚔️ Welcome to the NovelSmith Demo Vault!

Hello, writer! 👋 Welcome to your safe sandbox. 

This vault comes pre-loaded with dummy chapters, characters, and the **NovelSmith** plugin fully configured. **You cannot break anything here**, so feel free to click, drag, type, and experiment with all the features before using it on your actual manuscript.

To get the full NovelSmith experience, please follow this quick 5-minute interactive tutorial!

---

## 🛠️ Step 0: Open the Command Center
Before we start, make sure you can see the NovelSmith control panel.
1. Look at the **Right Sidebar** of your Obsidian window.
2. Click the **"NovelSmith Panel"** icon (it looks like a Kanban board/list).
3. Open any file in the `MyBook` folder (e.g., `01_Chapter_One.md`). You should now see the Structure View light up with Scene Cards!

---

## 🪄 Step 1: The Magic Outline (Drag & Drop)
Let's organize your plot without touching the text.
1. Make sure you have `01_Chapter_One.md` open.
2. In the right panel, look at the **📑 Outline** tab.
3. **Try this:** Click and hold a Scene Card (e.g., 🎬 *The Hero Wakes Up*), and drag it below another scene.
4. Watch the main editor—the text reorganizes itself instantly! Don't worry, your `Ctrl+Z` (Undo) history is perfectly preserved.
5. **Change Colors:** Hover over a Scene Card in the panel, click the little 🎨 palette icon, and change it to Red (Conflict) or Blue (Calm). Notice how the callout block in your text updates automatically!

---

## 📚 Step 2: Scrivenings Mode (The Game Changer)
Tired of opening 10 different files to read a single arc? Let's merge them.
1. Click on the `MyBook` folder in your file explorer.
2. Open any chapter inside it.
3. In the NovelSmith Panel, click the purple **"📚 Scrivenering"** button.
4. Select all chapters in the popup and hit **Start**.
5. **Boom!** You are now in a temporary, unified draft (`NSmith_Scrivenering.md`). Scroll through it. Read it seamlessly. 
6. **Try editing:** Change a sentence anywhere in this long document.
7. **The Sync:** When you're done editing, click the **"💾 Sync & close"** button in the panel. Watch as NovelSmith intelligently distributes your edits back to their original chapter files and safely archives the draft.

---

## 💾 Step 3: Atomic Version Control (Time Travel)
Let's back up a single scene without copying the whole file.
1. Open `02_Chapter_Two.md`.
2. Click anywhere inside the text of the first scene.
3. Go to the **🕰️ Backup** tab in the NovelSmith panel.
4. Click **"💾 Save current version"** and give it a name (e.g., "Before the big fight").
5. Now, delete a whole paragraph from that scene in the editor.
6. Look back at the panel, find your saved version, click **"Recover"**, and confirm. Your scene is instantly restored without affecting the rest of the chapter!

---

## ✍️ Step 4: Try the Writing Aids
NovelSmith comes with built-in tools for the editing phase.
1. Press `Ctrl+P` (or `Cmd+P`) to open the Command Palette.
2. Type **"NovelSmith: Dialogue Mode"** and hit Enter. Watch all the descriptive text fade away, letting you focus entirely on the spoken words. (Run the command again to turn it off).
3. Type **"NovelSmith: Redundant Mode"**. Notice how filler words (like "actually", "basically") light up in red. (You can customize these words in `_Backstage/Aids/RedundantList.md`).

---

## 🚀 Ready for your own novel?

If you loved this demo and want to install NovelSmith in your actual writing vault:
1. Copy the `novelsmith` plugin folder from this demo's `.obsidian/plugins/` directory into your own vault.
2. Enable it in your settings.
3. Go to **Settings > NovelSmith**, set your **Dedicated Writing Folder** (e.g., "MyEpicNovel"), and click **"🚀 Initialize Now"**. 

Happy writing, and may the words flow effortlessly! 🖋️
