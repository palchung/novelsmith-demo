# 🌍 NovelSmith Auto Wiki: The Ultimate Beginner's Guide

Welcome to Auto Wiki! This feature is designed to turn your scattered character names and locations or others custom item in scene card into a fully connected, interactive World Bible—automatically. 

Let's walk through it step-by-step, assuming you've never used it before. 
## Step 1: Tell NovelSmith What to Look For (Settings)
First, we need to teach NovelSmith how your World Bible is organized.
1. Open Obsidian **Settings** and go to **NovelSmith**.
2. Scroll down to the **Auto wiki categories** section.
3. Click **+ Add wiki category**.
4. **Category name:** What do you call this in your scene cards? 
   * *Pro Tip (Merging categories):* Do you use both `POV` and `Players` in your scene cards to track characters? You can combine them! Just type: `Character, POV, Players` separated by commas. NovelSmith will now treat all of these as "Characters".
5. **Storage folder:** Where should these notes live? Type a path like `MyBook/World/Characters`.

## Step 2: Create Your Custom Template
You don't want your new character notes to be completely blank, right?
1. In the same settings page, next to the category you just created, click **Generate Template**.
2. NovelSmith will create a template file in `_Backstage/Templates` and open it for you.
3. Edit this file! Add `# {{WikiName}}` at the top, and maybe add sections like `## Backstory` or `## Appearance`. Whenever Auto Wiki creates a new character, it will use this exact layout!

## Step 3: Unleash the Auto Wiki Magic
Now for the fun part.
1. Open one of your chapters. 
2. In a scene card's attributes, type some names. E.g., `> - POV:: Alice, Bob`.
3. Go to the right NovelSmith panel, click the **🔧 Tools** icon (the wrench), and click **Auto wiki** (the book icon).
4. *Watch the magic:* NovelSmith instantly turns "Alice, Bob" into `[[Alice]], [[Bob]]`. If those files didn't exist, it automatically creates them in your chosen folder using your custom template!

## Step 4: The Interactive "Info" Panel
You no longer need to switch tabs to remember who a character is.
1. While writing a scene, look at the **Info** tab in the right panel.
2. It automatically reads the attributes of the scene your cursor is on. 
3. Your characters (Alice, Bob) will appear as colorful, clickable buttons (Chips)!
4. Click the **Folder Icon** next to them to browse every single character in your World Bible.

## Step 5: Live Editing (The Best Part!)
Need to update Alice's eye color while you're writing?
1. Click "Alice" in the Info panel to read her lore.
2. Click the **✏️ Pencil Icon** next to her name.
3. Alice's note will open in a split-screen next to your novel.
4. Start typing in Alice's note. Pause for a second, and watch the Info panel on the right *instantly update* to show your new text! 

You are now a Worldbuilding Master!
